Chest Pain Type

Typical Angina (1)

Asymptomatic (4) or Atypical/Non-anginal — higher risk since silent or irregular pain may be dangerous.

Resting BP S (Blood Pressure)

90 – 120 mmHg

>130 mmHg → indicates hypertension, risk for heart attack or stroke.

Cholesterol

Amount of fat in blood (mg/dL) < 200 mg/dL is desirable

>240 mg/dL is high risk for clogged arteries

Fasting Blood Sugar

Blood glucose level after fasting (>8 hrs) < 100 mg/dL (normal)

>120 mg/dL (flagged as diabetic or pre-diabetic, risky)

Resting ECG

Heart’s electrical pattern at rest Normal (0)

ST-T abnormality (1) or LVH (2) → electrical or muscular heart problems

Max Heart Rate

Highest heart rate during exercise Depends on age: 220 – age

Too low for age may show poor heart function

Exercise Angina

Chest pain during physical activity No (0) — heart handles exertion well

Yes (1) — common in blocked arteries

Oldpeak (ST Depression)

Depression in ST segment during stress < 1 mm

>2 mm — may indicate ischemia (low oxygen in heart)
